Panna Cotta

Ingredients

1 1/2 cups whole milk (see Ingredient Notes) 1 Tbsp. powdered gelatin 5-6 Tbsp. sugar 1 1/2 cups light or heavy cream 1 vanilla bean, split lengthwise Pinch salt

Instructions

Coat the inside of six ramekins or molds with non-flavored oil or a little butter, using a paper towel, so that the dishes are coated with just a light residue. Pour the milk into a 2-quart saucepan and scrape the seeds from the vanilla bean into the milk, along with the bean pod. Sprinkle the powdered gelatin evenly over top and allow it to "bloom" for 5 minutes or until the surface of the milk is wrinkled and the gelatin grains have begun to dissolve. warm the milk gently over low heat stirring frequently. If the milk begins to steam, remove the pan from the heat and let it cool down. Don't let it boil or scald. Add the sugar to the milk and stir until dissolved, then set the pan aside to steep for 20-30 minutes. Remove vanilla bean pod. Bring the milk in the sauce pan back up to where it is hot but not boiling and whisk in the cream and a pinch of salt. Divide the mixture evenly among the ramekins and put in the refrigerator to chill for 3-4 hours minimum. To unmold, run a thin knife carefully around the sides of a ramekin and dip the ramekins in warm for about 3-4 seconds. Invert the ramekin over the plate and shake gently to help the panna cotta fall out. Garnish with fresh fruit and serve immediately, or refrigerate, lightly covered, for up to 2 days.
